Cookie Policy

Last updated: February 21, 2026

This Cookie Policy explains how Zyntex ("we", "us", or "our") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies on our website (zyntex.app). By using our website, you consent to the use of cookies as described in this policy.

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device (computer, smartphone, or tablet) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, remember your preferences, and provide information to website owners.

Similar technologies include web beacons, pixels, local storage, and session storage, which function in comparable ways to cookies. This policy covers all such technologies collectively referred to as "cookies."

2. Types of Cookies We Use

Category Purpose Duration
Essential Required for the website to function. Includes session management, security tokens, and load balancing. Cannot be disabled. Session / 1 year
Functional Remember your preferences such as language selection and theme. Improve your experience without tracking. 1 year
Analytics Help us understand how visitors use the site (pages visited, time on site, referral sources). We use aggregated, anonymized data only. Up to 2 years
Marketing Used to deliver relevant advertising if you have consented. May include retargeting pixels from ad platforms. Up to 1 year

3. Specific Cookies We Use

  • Firebase: Authentication state, Firestore session persistence, and app functionality.
  • Google Analytics: Website traffic analysis (if enabled). Data is anonymized and aggregated.
  • Google Fonts: Font delivery — Google may set cookies when serving font files.
  • Local Storage: App preferences and cached data stored in your browser's local storage.

4. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies for the following purposes:

  • Keeping you signed in to your Zyntex account across sessions.
  • Remembering your language and display preferences.
  • Analyzing website performance and user behavior to improve our services.
  • Detecting and preventing fraudulent activity and security threats.
  • Measuring the effectiveness of our marketing campaigns.

5. Third-Party Cookies

Some cookies on our website are set by third-party services. We do not control these cookies and their use is governed by the respective third-party privacy policies:

  • Google / Firebase: Google Privacy Policy
  • Google Analytics: Google Analytics Cookie Policy

6. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You can control and manage cookies in several ways:

  • Browser Settings: Most browsers allow you to refuse, delete, or manage cookies through their settings. Refer to your browser's help documentation for instructions.
  • Opt-out Tools: For Google Analytics, you can install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on.
  • Cookie Banners: When applicable, our cookie consent banner allows you to accept or decline non-essential cookies.

Please note that disabling certain cookies may affect the functionality of the Zyntex website or app. Essential cookies cannot be disabled as they are necessary for the service to function.

7. Do Not Track

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature that signals websites not to track your activity. As there is no universally accepted standard for DNT responses, our website does not currently respond to DNT signals. However, you can use the cookie management options above to limit tracking.

We may update this Cookie Policy from time to time to reflect changes in technology, law, or our practices. We will post the updated policy on this page with a revised "Last updated" date. Your continued use of the website after changes are posted constitutes your acceptance of the updated policy.
